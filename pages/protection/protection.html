National Commission for Women (NCW)

The NCW is a statutory body of the Government of India, established to protect and promote the interests of women. It investigates and examines all matters relating to the constitutional and legal safeguards for women.

  • Investigates complaints and takes suo-motu notice of matters relating to women's rights
  • Reviews constitutional and legal safeguards for women
  • Participates and advises on planning process of socio-economic development
  • Evaluates progress of development for women
  • Inspects jails, remand homes, and women's institutions
  • Calls for special studies on issues of discrimination and atrocities

Important Legal Provisions

Understanding laws that protect women's rights in India

IPC Section 354

Assault or Criminal Force to Women

Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to two years, or with fine, or with both.

IPC Section 498A

Cruelty by Husband or Relatives

Whoever, being the husband or relative of the husband of a woman, subjects such woman to cruelty shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to fine. Cruelty includes harassment for dowry demands.

IPC Section 509

Word, Gesture or Act to Insult Modesty

Whoever, intending to insult the modesty of any woman, utters any word, makes any sound or gesture, or exhibits any object, intending that such word or sound shall be heard, or that such gesture or object shall be seen, by such woman, shall be punished with simple imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, and also with fine.

Domestic Violence Act 2005

Protection of Women from Domestic Violence

This Act provides for more effective protection of the rights of women guaranteed under the Constitution who are victims of violence of any kind occurring within the family. It covers physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse.
