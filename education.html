Education Schemes

Government Schemes for Women's Education

Women's education is a critical issue in India, and the Indian government has implemented several schemes aimed to improve the literacy rate of women and empower them to lead a better life.

Featured Scheme

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao is a central government scheme that helps girls throughout the nation. The primary purpose of this scheme is to save the child from social problems such as gender-based abortions and advance child education around the country.

This program was initially meant for districts considered to have a low sex ratio and successfully extended to other regions of the country. This is essentially an educational programme to help change societal attitudes and does not entail the immediate transfer of funds.

Key Objectives:
  • Prevention of gender-biased sex-selective elimination
  • Ensuring survival and protection of the girl child
  • Ensuring education and participation of the girl child

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana

Government of India backed savings scheme designed for parents of girl children. The scheme allows parents to set up a trust for their child's eventual schooling and marriage expenses.

  • Parents of girl children under 10 can open account
  • Minimum deposit of Rs.250 per annum, maximum Rs.1.5 lakhs
  • Tax exemption available on deposits and withdrawals
  • Maximum tenure of 21 years from opening

Mukhyamantri Rajshri Yojana

Launched in Rajasthan, offering monetary benefits to parents of girl children from birth till higher education.

  • Rs.2500 at birth of girl child
  • Rs.2500 after completion of one year with vaccinations
  • Rs.4000 at admission in Grade I
  • Rs.5000 when entering Grade VI
  • Rs.11000 when entering Grade XI
