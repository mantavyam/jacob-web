Prevention of Violence Against Women

The Constitution of India not only grants equality to women but also empowers the State to adopt measures of positive discrimination in favour of women for neutralizing the cumulative socio-economic, educational and political disadvantages faced by them.

Your Rights Under Indian Law

IPC Sections

  • Section 376: Rape
  • Sections 363-373: Kidnapping & Abduction
  • Section 302/304-B: Dowry Deaths
  • Section 498-A: Cruelty (mental & physical torture)
  • Section 354: Molestation
  • Section 509: Sexual Harassment
  • Section 366-B: Importation of girls (up to 21 years)

Special Laws

  • Commission of Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987
  • Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961
  • Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986
  • Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956
  • Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005
  • Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013
